## Literature

This is my literature database, how I organize the literature I've consumed. I try my best to keep it updated because there's a lot of times when I'm discussing ideas and need a specific paper reference, but I'm still working on regularly updating it as I read. 

I discover new papers on Twitter and also reading ArXiv like the news every day. Specifically, I browse through the [cs.AI](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/recent), [cs.CL](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.CL/recent), and [cs.LG](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.LG/recent) reading through titles and abstracts. The more interesting papers I'll save for a more in depth read later. Obviously there are more categories on ArXiv in my domain than just these three, but this is a good subsample to start with and when I have more time I'll do a comprehensive sweep using a specific search. 

My reading process can be broken down into 5 components:
1. Discovery -- through Twitter or ArXiv as described above.
2. Filter by title and abstract -- is this empirically or theoretically interesting to me?
3. Filter by results -- skip the introduction and methods and jump to the results table. If the results are valid e.g. working with T5 instead of BERT for language tasks in this day and age, I'll keep it otherwise it's filtered out. 
4. Read through once and record it in the literature database (not a habit just yet, but I'm working on it). 
5. Re-read -- sometimes I'll just randomly think about a paper in the shower or something and that'll inspire me to re-read it.
